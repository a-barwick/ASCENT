# Product direction

ASCENT puts the player in charge of the industrial program that makes an
impossible mission real.

Every launch, deployment, habitat, orbital facility, or emergency response is a
major commitment. The player coordinates suppliers, hardware, transport,
engineering evidence, testing, schedule, financing, political capital, and the
people whose names go on the final decision.

## Core principles

- **The mission is the point.** Manufacturing, procurement, logistics, finance,
  and coordination matter because they determine whether a consequential
  mission can be attempted and delivered.
- **Risk has a history.** A technical warning should emerge from earlier choices
  such as supplier selection, compressed qualification, reused hardware, waived
  testing, or an overloaded program.
- **Decisions move several pressures at once.** Cost, schedule, evidence,
  confidence, mission exposure, customer tolerance, reputation, and rival
  position create the tradeoff.
- **Information is a resource.** Tests, inspections, independent review, and
  supplier records let the player buy confidence, discover danger, or justify a
  waiver.
- **Commitment is accountable.** Readiness gates, named sign-offs, preserved
  objections, configuration history, and a final GO or SCRUB make ownership
  visible.
- **Competition is operational.** Rivals contest mandates, launch windows,
  qualified suppliers, scarce materials, skilled teams, insurance capacity, and
  public confidence.
- **Consequences should persist.** Success builds capability and leverage.
  Failure creates claims, investigations, debt, lost confidence, and openings
  for competitors. A scrub can preserve the company while surrendering an
  opportunity.
- **The interface is mission control.** It should prioritize the active hold,
  evidence, dependencies, readiness, time pressure, and accountable decision
  instead of presenting disconnected management screens.

## Current product reference

The repository currently implements ORPHEUS-1, a self-contained Svelte proof of
concept focused on one readiness decision and the final commitment gate. It is
not yet a persistent campaign or multiplayer game.

For the intended campaign loop, the exact ORPHEUS-1 flow, and the boundary
between the current UI and future systems, see
[Program campaign](program-campaign.md).
